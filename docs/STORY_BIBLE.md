# Nuke Raider — Story Bible

> **Golden source for all narrative, character, and story arc decisions.**
> Dialog issues, mission briefings, and NPC writing must reference this document.

---

## World & Tone

Post-apocalyptic wasteland. Roads are power — whoever controls them controls survival.

**Factions:**
- **Road Guild** — dominant courier/enforcer network. Ruthless, organized, reach everywhere.
- **Scrap Kings** — opportunist bandits. No ideology, only profit. Will work with anyone.
- **Old World Corp** — pre-war tech hoarders. Mostly myth until Act 3.

**Tone:** Gritty and sparse + dry humor. Judas Priest energy — every word earns its place. Think *Road Warrior* with occasional one-liners.

---

## Characters

### RUNNER (player)

Former Road Guild courier, gone rogue. Good at one thing: driving. Dry, economical with words. No cause until now. Doesn't ask questions he doesn't need answers to.

**Voice:** Speaks in fragments. Never explains himself unless pressed. No apologies, no monologues. If he's got three words for it, he uses two.

**Sample lines:**
> [silence]
> "Your rig now."
> "Fine."
> "Doesn't matter."
> "Let's go."

---

### VALE (the daughter)

Guild boss's daughter. Street-smart, hostile, agenda-driven. She had her own escape plan — hiding in the prototype rig. Runner accidentally hijacked it. She chose no one. Warms to Runner exactly once, when it matters.

**Voice:** Sharp and agenda-driven. Uses questions as weapons ("What do you think you're doing?" not "Why did you do that?"). Knows more than she lets on. Gives information in pieces, never all at once.

**Sample lines — hostile phase (Act 1):**
> "Don't."
> "You didn't need to know that."
> "Just drive."

**Sample lines — cooperation phase (Act 2):**
> "Northeast. Don't ask why."
> "...better than I thought."

**The moment it matters (Act 3):**
> "I need you to trust me. Once."

---

### BOSS CADE (antagonist)

Guild boss, Vale's father. Never appears in person — only through NPC references and mission context. All threat, no screen budget.

## 3-Act Story Arc

### Act 1 — Run *(Rust Town / Guild territory)*

Runner flees with the stolen rig. Vale is hostile, gives nothing. Missions: blast through Guild checkpoints. Her agenda surfaces in fragments — she's not just running from the Guild, she's heading *toward* something.

**Missions:**
- Checkpoint Run (RACE) — tutorial, blast through Guild checkpoints
- Convoy Break (COMBAT_RUN) — first combat; Vale's first word: "Drive."

### Act 2 — The Road *(Scrap Kings territory)*

Uneasy truce. Vale starts cooperating out of survival necessity, not trust. Reveal: she found Old World Corp maps in her father's office. The safe haven is real — she knows exactly where it is.

**Missions:**
- Border Sprint (RACE) — race through no-man's-land into Scrap Kings territory
- Old World Score (COMBAT_RUN) — fight to retrieve map fragment from convoy; Vale reveals the maps

### Act 3 — The Nuke *(Old World Corp facility)*

The safe haven is an Old World Corp weapons facility. Her father knew. The Guild was its enforcer. Vale was being *delivered* there, not escaping. Final mission: Guild pursuit through the facility access road.

**Player choice ending:** destroy it or take it.

**Missions:**
- Facility Approach (COMBAT_RUN) — through the outer perimeter
- Final Gauntlet (COMBAT_RUN) — Guild pursuit to the facility core; ending triggers here

## Per-Hub NPC Roles

### Rust Town (Act 1)
- **Mechanic** — repairs, knows Guild patrol schedules, trades in parts
- **Trader** — buys/sells scrap, hears rumors from passing couriers
- **Drifter** — ex-Guild, knows the road, won't say how he knows it

### Scrap Yard (Act 2)
- **Fixer** — Scrap Kings contact, arranges the uneasy truce
- **Scout** — knows Old World Corp territory, sells map fragments
- **Wrecker** — muscle, has seen the facility from the outside

### The Gateway (Act 3)
No hub NPCs. Just Vale and the road ahead.

---

## NPC Voice Guide

**Generic NPCs (Mechanic, Trader, Drifter, etc.):** World-weary. Everyone has an angle. Information costs something. Short sentences, no trust given freely.

**Sample:**
> "Runs. Mostly."
> "Guild was through here two days back. That's all I know."

---

## Mission Naming Convention

- **RACE missions** — named after the movement: "Checkpoint Run", "Border Sprint"
- **COMBAT_RUN missions** — named after the objective: "Convoy Break", "Old World Score", "Facility Gauntlet"

## Delivery Constraints

- All story delivery via NPC hub dialog + mission briefings. No mid-game cutscenes.
- Boss Cade never appears on screen — presence only through dialog and mission context.
- Vale has one significant line per act milestone. Less is more.
